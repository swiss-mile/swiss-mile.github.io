The Robot

An all-rounder on legs and wheels

An ideal mobile robot must excel in mobility, overcoming obstacles with speed and efficiency while carrying substantial payloads—benchmarks that existing robots have yet to fully meet. Widespread adoption has been hindered by limitations in real-world reasoning. To overcome these challenges, we have developed an end-to-end solution that combines wheeled-legged robots with our advanced embodied AI. Our robots can walk, drive, stand upright, open doors, and manipulate packages, showcasing the versatility of our approach in addressing real-world challenges such as mobility, autonomy, and manipulation. With this integration, we are driving the next wave of automation, advancing beyond the era of static robotic arms.

Use cases

We have expanded our reach across sectors such as security, monitoring, construction, and logistics to address the growing global challenge of labor shortages. Our focus is on delivering two versatile solutions tailored to different use cases: one engineered for seamless transport of goods, and another designed for advanced monitoring capabilities. These state-of-the-art solutions not only enhance cost efficiency but also prioritize worker safety and well-being by alleviating the burden of repetitive tasks and providing valuable operational insights.

Security

The robot patrols and reports anomalies. In emergency scenarios, it assesses the situation before the security team arrives.

Monitoring

The robot, equipped with multi-modal sensors, excels at detecting anomalies, thereby minimizing facility downtime.

Construction

Through routine quality and progress tracking, the construction manager can automatically spot safety hazards and construction mistakes early.

Logistics

General-purpose platform for urban environments, allowing various logistic applications by carrying goods, materials and tools.

Our Embodied AI

Embodied AI

At ETH Zurich's Robotic Systems Lab, the founders spent six years pioneering research in robotic intelligence and wheeled-legged robots, focusing on robot design, mobility, autonomy, and manipulation. For more details on their research, see the videos here. At Swiss-Mile, we are further advancing and commercializing embodied AI to address real-world robotics challenges. By integrating reinforcement and supervised learning into a unified framework, we enable robots to autonomously learn from scratch and adapt based on real-world deployments. This approach allows us to generate and scale novel robotic behaviors, continually refining them through data collected from both real-world environments and synthetic scenarios. Our versatile AI framework can bring any robotic system to life, independent of its embodiment.
