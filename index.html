The Robot

The future of robotic mobility!

An ideal mobile robot should excel in mobility, overcoming obstacles with speed and efficiency while carrying substantial payloads. Existing robots fall short of meeting all these criteria. Moreover, their widespread adoption faces challenges due to limitations in real-world reasoning. Addressing these limitations, Swiss-Mile combines the wheeled-legged robot of Unitree Robotics with our embodied AI to commercialize our end-to-end solution.

Within the Robotic Systems Lab of ETH Zurich, the founders dedicated six years to pioneering research in robotic intelligence and wheeled-legged robots. In the following videos, the founders built on a third-party robot and further customized it strictly for research purposes.

Robust locomotion and autonomous navigation

We introduce an autonomous navigation system developed for wheeled-legged robots, designed for fast and efficient navigation within large urban environments. Driven by neural network policies, our simple, unified control system enables smooth gait transitions, smart navigation planning, and highly responsive obstacle avoidance in populated urban environments.

Transformer opens doors and manipulates packages

We introduce an intrinsically motivated RL approach for learning complex locomotion and manipulation tasks, aiming to reduce the need for extensive task-specific engineering. The proposed method encodes the desired task in a single sparse reward (+1 for task achievement) and leverages intrinsic motivation to guide exploration towards this reward signal.
